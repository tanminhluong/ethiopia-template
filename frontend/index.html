Top Destinations

Lalibela

Home to the famous rock-hewn churches, Lalibela is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Ethiopia. The churches were carved out of solid rock in the 12th century and are considered to be one of the world's most impressive architectural wonders.

Simien Mountains National Park

Located in the northern part of Ethiopia, the Simien Mountains are a breathtaking mountain range that offers incredible trekking opportunities. The park is home to rare wildlife species like the Ethiopian wolf and the gelada baboon, as well as stunning landscapes and waterfalls.

Danakil Depression

One of the world's most inhospitable places, the Danakil Depression has become a fascinating destination among travelers. This vast desert landscape offers colorful hot springs, salt pans, and other unique geological formations.

Gondar

Also known as the "Camelot of Africa," Gondar is a historic city in northern Ethiopia known for its stunning castles and medieval architecture. The city was once the capital of Ethiopia and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Lake Tana and the Blue Nile Falls

Located in the northwest of Ethiopia, Lake Tana is the source of the Blue Nile and is surrounded by stunning landscapes and historic monasteries. Visitors can take boat tours on the lake and visit the nearby Blue Nile Falls, one of Ethiopia's most breathtaking natural wonders.

Travel In The High Season

Temperature: 11 - 24°C (January to March)
This is the best time to visit Ethiopia since you will be able to tour historical sites and natural places without having to deal with crowds. However, there may be rain in specific regions, such as Addis Abeba; travelers may be walking in the Simien or Bale mountains at this time.

Low Season

13 - 23°C

April
to
September

Travel In The Low Season

Temperature: 13 - 23°C (April to September)
This is the highest rainy season in the nation, often known as Kiremt season. If you want to visit Simien or Bale mountains, avoid visiting at this time due to the high level of rain. In contrast, travelers might visit these places after July.
